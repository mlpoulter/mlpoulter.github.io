Martin Poulter

Open Culture, Open Data, Open Education

I work in the intersection of open education, open culture, and open data. I create and deliver customised training for academics, librarians, and other experts for working with open projects such as Wikipedia and Wikidata to get the maximum benefit in terms of research impact, public engagement, and educational resources.

Training and consultancy

My clients have included the Khalili Collections, University of Oxford GLAM Division, the Voltaire Foundation, Jisc, Coventry University (for Wikipedia/Wikidata training), University of Gloucestershire (for social media training), and BBC Research (for psychological expertise).

I am a certified Lead Trainer for Wikimedia UK. As a volunteer, I have delivered training sessions and other events for the Institute of Physics, British Psychological Society, the National Institute of Medical Research, the University of Leicester, University of Edinburgh, and others.

My Wikimedia UK profile lists all the wiki training events I have been involved in.

Background

I was educated at the University of Oxford (BA Philosophy and Psychology) and the University of Bristol (PhD Philosophy).

My doctoral thesis, "Value and Belief", applied decision theory to issues of rational and irrational belief.

Publications

My Wikipedia user page lists my Wikimedia-relevant publications. My ORCID profile has a broader list but is updated less frequently. Some of my articles about Wikidata are available on Medium.

Public speaking

I have given more than forty talks to skeptics groups, student groups, and Edinburgh Fringe audiences about topics including cult recruitment, cognitive psychology and critical thinking.

I was the opening keynote speaker at Repository Fringe 2016 and at the University of Oxford's Open Access Week 2019
