Martin Poulter

Open Culture, Open Data, Open Education

I work in the intersection of open education, open culture, and open data. I create and deliver customised training for academics, librarians, and other experts in working with open projects such as Wikipedia and Wikidata to get the maximum benefit in terms of research impact, public engagement, and interactive educational resources.

Training and consultancy

My clients have included the Khalili Collections, University of Oxford GLAM Division, the Voltaire Foundation, Jisc, Coventry University (for Wikipedia/Wikidata training), University of Gloucestershire (for social media training), and BBC Research (for psychological expertise).

I am a certified Lead Trainer for Wikimedia UK. As a volunteer, I have delivered training sessions and other events for the Institute of Physics, British Psychological Society, the National Institute of Medical Research, the University of Leicester, University of Edinburgh

My Wikimedia UK profile lists all the wiki training events I have been involved in

Background

I was educated at the University of Oxford (BA Philosophy and Psychology) and the University of Bristol (PhD Philosophy). My PhD thesis, "Value and Belief" applied decision theory to issues of rational and irrational belief.
